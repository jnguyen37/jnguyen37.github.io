Phase 3

02-18-2019 / 18:26:00

Phase 3

Phase one and phase two are now complete! Character designs are in the final stages of development. A working version is available. Phase three completes storyline and include all the clues needed to solve the mystery case. The cluebook is implemented to allow users to organize the clues and easily decide who the antagonist may be by previewing the information. Extra trim and netralizing powerups for enemies have been added as well.

Framed is a 2D puzzle mystery game where the player helps the main protagonist Tora the Tiger to prove his innocence. The main objective for this game is to solve a series of puzzles and riddles to find items located throughout each level of the game leading to figuring out who was the suspect that was responsible for the crime that was committed leading to your embezzlement. To make the game more challenging and interesting we planned to implement various features like player movement, power ups system, which is a fireball used in the game to knock out the enemy AI, clue book, teleport, permission system, mini map, disable enemy, health system, safe area, AI system, inventory and Disguise in order to make it more of a unique experience. Based on our initial planned design we implement all the features except the Mini map. Overall, the mini map with top down view of the game doesn’t seem that necessary for our game type. To save more time and effort of developers we mostly invest more time trying to implement more logical and interesting clues in each and every levels of the game. We have a total of six levels. In each level, there will be different clues that the player has to use in order to figure out which one of the suspects in the game are guilty, and win the game if they solve the mystery correctly otherwise it will be game over.

Originally, we were going to allow the player to freely choose where ever they wanted to go, but due to time limitations, we had to changed it to just a fixed route where the player is only limited to going on a linear route. Also, for the story, we were originally have multiple versions to where each playthrough would bring a new experience, but had to limit it to only one. For example, when the player replays the game, not only that the rules will be the same, but it will also lead them to different routes where the criminal will be a different character comparing to their last playthrough. Therefore, the overall final version we have for our game is that we ended up scrapping a few ideas we have originally planned for our game, but still delivered a final product we are still very satisfied with. Overall, the lesson learned when designing a game with time constraints was differentiating between what would be stretch goals versus major or core game components. While completing stretch goals are nice, having core mechanics down is key to ensuring that the product delivered will be satisfactory to the requirements.
